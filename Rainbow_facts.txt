A rainbow is a meteorological phenomenon that is caused by reflection, refraction and dispersion of light in water droplets resulting
in a spectrum of light appearing in the sky. It takes the form of a multicoloured circular arc. 
Rainbows caused by sunlight always appear in the section of sky directly opposite the sun.

Rainbow consists of 7 colors called VIBGYOR
V - Violet
I - Indigo
B - Blue
G - Green
Y - Yellow
O - Orange
R - Red

Now here are some facts about rainbow.

1) The arc of the rainbow has violet tone for the inner areas. On the other hand, the outer side features the red tone.
2) A double rainbow may occur too. The inner part of the arc features the red hue. The primary arc is seen inside the second arc.
3) Rainbow is an optical illusion. If you view a rainbow, it is not possible for you to grab it or even touch. It is not a real object.
4) The perfect time to spot the rainbow is during the early evening and morning. 
The latter one is in the western sky, while the former one is in the eastern sky.
5) The common places to spot the rainbow are located closer to the fountains or waterfalls.
